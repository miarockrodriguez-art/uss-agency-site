Proactive Protection Against Workplace Threats

Workplace violence is one of the leading causes of occupational injury and death in the United States. Threats can come from current or former employees, domestic situations that follow employees to work, disgruntled customers, or random acts of violence. U.S.S. Agency provides specialized armed and unarmed security officers trained in workplace violence prevention, de-escalation, and emergency response for businesses throughout Orlando and Central Florida.

As a licensed security agency in Florida, U.S.S. Agency deploys officers who understand the warning signs of escalating behavior, the legal framework governing workplace security in Florida, and the tactical response protocols necessary to protect employees when prevention fails. Our workplace violence security programs are designed to address threats before they become incidents and to respond decisively when incidents occur.

Termination and High-Risk HR Event Security

Employee terminations, layoffs, disciplinary actions, and restructuring events carry elevated risk for workplace violence. U.S.S. Agency provides on-site armed security officers during high-risk HR events to protect management, HR staff, and remaining employees. Our officers maintain a discreet but accessible presence during termination meetings, escort separated employees off the premises, monitor parking areas, and remain on site during the transition period when retaliation risk is highest.

We coordinate with HR departments and legal counsel to develop event-specific security plans that address the individual threat assessment, communication protocols, and follow-up monitoring necessary to manage the risk period effectively.

Threat Assessment and Monitoring

Many workplace violence incidents are preceded by warning signs including threats, intimidation, stalking, obsessive behavior, and documented grievances. U.S.S. Agency works with employers to establish security protocols that respond to reported threats with appropriate measures including increased security presence, access restriction for identified individuals, escort services for targeted employees, and coordination with law enforcement for credible threats of violence.

Domestic Violence Spillover Protection

When employees are victims of domestic violence, their workplace can become a target because it is a predictable location where the perpetrator knows the victim will be present. U.S.S. Agency provides security services that protect employees dealing with domestic violence situations including entrance monitoring for identified individuals, parking lot escorts, and rapid response if an abuser appears at the workplace. Our officers handle these situations with discretion and compassion while maintaining the safety of all employees in the facility.
