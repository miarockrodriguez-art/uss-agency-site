Protecting High-Value Vehicle Inventory

Car dealerships represent millions of dollars in vehicle inventory spread across open lots that are inherently difficult to secure. Vehicle theft, catalytic converter theft, tire and wheel theft, vandalism, and parts stripping are constant threats that cost the automotive industry billions annually. U.S.S. Agency provides armed and unarmed security guards for car dealerships throughout Orlando and Central Florida, delivering a visible security presence that protects your inventory, your property, and your bottom line.

As a licensed security agency in Florida, U.S.S. Agency understands the automotive retail environment and the specific threats that dealerships face. Our officers are trained to patrol vehicle lots effectively, monitor for suspicious activity, and respond to incidents in ways that protect inventory without disrupting legitimate customer activity during business hours.

Overnight Lot Security

The majority of dealership theft and vandalism occurs after hours when the sales floor is closed and the lot is unattended. U.S.S. Agency provides overnight security officers who patrol the entire lot perimeter, monitor all vehicle rows, check service bays and parts departments, and verify that all access points are secured. Our guards use randomized patrol patterns and maintain constant communication with our operations center to ensure continuous coverage throughout the night.

Catalytic converter theft has become an epidemic in the automotive industry, with organized crews targeting dealership lots where dozens of vehicles sit unprotected overnight. A single night of catalytic converter theft can cost a dealership tens of thousands of dollars in parts, labor, and lost sales. Our overnight security officers specifically monitor for the tools, vehicles, and behavior patterns associated with converter theft operations.

Showroom and Customer Area Security

During business hours, dealership security takes on a customer-service dimension. U.S.S. Agency officers maintain a professional, approachable presence in and around the showroom, service department, and customer waiting areas. Our guards monitor for shoplifting in parts departments, manage test drive key control procedures, and provide a visible deterrent against aggressive or disruptive behavior from visitors.

Key Control and Vehicle Movement Monitoring

Unauthorized vehicle access and joy rides are ongoing risks at dealerships where keys are accessible and vehicles are ready to drive. U.S.S. Agency officers support key control procedures by monitoring key lockboxes, verifying authorization for vehicle movements, and tracking test drive departures and returns. This oversight reduces the risk of vehicle theft, unauthorized use, and accidents involving dealership inventory.
