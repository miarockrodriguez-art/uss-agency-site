Protecting High-Value Vehicle Inventory

Car dealerships represent millions of dollars in vehicle inventory spread across open lots that are inherently difficult to secure. Vehicle theft, catalytic converter theft, tire and wheel theft, vandalism, and parts stripping are constant threats that cost the automotive industry billions annually. U.S.S. Agency provides armed and unarmed security guards for car dealerships throughout Orlando and Central Florida, delivering a visible security presence that protects your inventory, your property, and your bottom line.

As a licensed security agency in Florida, U.S.S. Agency understands the automotive retail environment and the specific threats that dealerships face. Our officers are trained to patrol vehicle lots effectively, monitor for suspicious activity, and respond to incidents in ways that protect inventory without disrupting legitimate customer activity during business hours.

Overnight Lot Security

The majority of dealership theft and vandalism occurs after hours when the sales floor is closed and the lot is unattended. U.S.S. Agency provides overnight security officers who patrol the entire lot perimeter, monitor all vehicle rows, check service bays and parts departments, and verify that all access points are secured. Our guards use randomized patrol patterns and maintain constant communication with our operations center to ensure continuous coverage throughout the night.

Catalytic converter theft has become an epidemic in the automotive industry, with organized crews targeting dealership lots where dozens of vehicles sit unprotected overnight. A single night of catalytic converter theft can cost a dealership tens of thousands of dollars in parts, labor, and lost sales. Our overnight security officers specifically monitor for the tools, vehicles, and behavior patterns associated with converter theft operations.

Showroom and Customer Area Security

During business hours, dealership security takes on a customer-service dimension. U.S.S. Agency officers maintain a professional, approachable presence in and around the showroom, service department, and customer waiting areas. Our guards monitor for shoplifting in parts departments, manage test drive key control procedures, and provide a visible deterrent against aggressive or disruptive behavior from visitors.

Key Control and Vehicle Movement Monitoring

Unauthorized vehicle access and joy rides are ongoing risks at dealerships where keys are accessible and vehicles are ready to drive. U.S.S. Agency officers support key control procedures by monitoring key lockboxes, verifying authorization for vehicle movements, and tracking test drive departures and returns. This oversight reduces the risk of vehicle theft, unauthorized use, and accidents involving dealership inventory.

Why Choose U.S.S. Agency for Dealership Security

Not all security providers understand the automotive retail environment. U.S.S. Agency has built its dealership security program around the operational realities that general-purpose guard companies overlook. Our officers know the difference between a customer browsing after hours and a threat approaching inventory. They understand that dealership lots have blind spots created by vehicle rows, that service bays contain tools and equipment worth targeting, and that the flow of customers, vendors, and delivery drivers creates access challenges that require experienced judgment.

Every officer deployed to a dealership account receives site-specific orientation covering the lot layout, camera coverage zones, key storage locations, alarm panel operations, and emergency contact protocols. This preparation ensures that our guards are operational from the first shift, not spending weeks learning the property while your inventory sits exposed. We assign dedicated officers to dealership accounts whenever possible, building familiarity with the property, staff, and daily routines that improves security effectiveness over time.

Our Process

U.S.S. Agency follows a structured approach to dealership security that begins with a comprehensive site assessment. Our operations team evaluates your lot layout, identifies vulnerability points, reviews existing security infrastructure such as cameras and lighting, and assesses the specific threat profile your dealership faces based on location, vehicle inventory value, and incident history. From this assessment, we develop a tailored security plan that specifies officer positioning, patrol routes, shift schedules, and communication protocols.

Once the plan is approved, we select officers whose experience and temperament match the assignment, conduct site-specific training, and deploy with full operational readiness. Throughout the engagement, our management team conducts regular quality checks, reviews shift reports for anomalies, and adjusts the security plan as conditions change. Clients receive direct access to our operations team and can request plan modifications at any time.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do your officers carry firearms on dealership lots? We offer both armed and unarmed options depending on your dealership's risk profile and preference. Many dealerships opt for armed officers during overnight shifts and unarmed officers during business hours to maintain a customer-friendly atmosphere.

Can your guards operate our alarm and camera systems? Yes. Our officers are trained on common commercial alarm panels and can be trained on your specific system during site orientation. They can arm, disarm, and reset systems as part of their duties.

How quickly can you deploy officers to our dealership? For standard engagements, we can deploy within 48 to 72 hours of contract execution. Emergency or urgent deployments can often be arranged within 24 hours depending on officer availability. We maintain a roster of qualified officers who are prepared for rapid deployment to meet urgent client needs.

Do you provide coverage for special sales events and tent sales? Absolutely. We provide supplemental officers for special events, weekend sales, tent sales, and any period where increased traffic or extended hours create additional security requirements. Our event officers integrate seamlessly with your regular security team and are briefed on the specific layout and traffic patterns associated with the event.
