Securing Active Construction Sites

Construction sites are high-value targets for theft, vandalism, and trespassing. Heavy equipment, copper wiring, building materials, and tools left on site overnight represent hundreds of thousands of dollars in assets that are often protected by nothing more than chain-link fencing. U.S.S. Agency provides armed and unarmed security guards for construction sites throughout Orlando and Central Florida, delivering 24/7 protection that prevents losses and keeps projects on schedule.

As a licensed security agency in Florida, U.S.S. Agency understands that construction site theft and vandalism can cause project delays, insurance premium increases, and budget overruns that far exceed the cost of professional security. Our officers are trained for the unique demands of construction environments, including site safety awareness, contractor access management, and after-hours perimeter security.

Equipment and Material Protection

Construction equipment theft is a multi-billion dollar industry nationwide. Excavators, generators, welding equipment, and specialized tools are frequently targeted because they are difficult to trace and easy to resell. U.S.S. Agency officers maintain a visible security presence on job sites that deters both opportunistic and organized theft operations. Our guards conduct equipment inventories at shift changes, verify that high-value items are properly secured at the end of each workday, and monitor staging areas where materials are delivered and stored.

Copper theft remains one of the most common crimes on construction sites, particularly during the rough-in phase when wiring is exposed and easily accessible. Our security officers pay specific attention to areas where copper, aluminum, and other valuable metals are installed or stored, reducing the risk of material theft that can set a project back weeks.

After-Hours and Overnight Security

The majority of construction site theft and vandalism occurs during nights, weekends, and holidays when workers are off site. U.S.S. Agency provides dedicated overnight security officers who patrol the perimeter, monitor access points, and respond to any unauthorized activity. Our guards use randomized patrol patterns to prevent predictability and maintain consistent communication with our operations center throughout the night.

Contractor and Visitor Access Management

Large construction projects involve dozens of subcontractors, suppliers, and inspectors accessing the site daily. U.S.S. Agency officers manage site entry points, verify contractor credentials, log deliveries, and ensure that only authorized personnel are present on the job site. This level of access control not only prevents theft but also supports OSHA compliance by ensuring that untrained individuals do not enter hazardous work zones.

Why Choose U.S.S. Agency for Construction Security

General-purpose security companies frequently assign officers to construction sites who have no experience with the construction environment. These guards do not understand the difference between a legitimate subcontractor arriving early and an unauthorized individual accessing the site. They are unfamiliar with the phases of construction and the shifting vulnerability points that each phase creates. U.S.S. Agency eliminates this gap by training our construction security officers on site-specific conditions including active work zones, phased access requirements, and the evolving layout of a project as it progresses from site prep through vertical construction and finish work.

We assign officers who understand that a construction site on Monday may look significantly different from the same site on Friday. Our guards adapt patrol routes, access control procedures, and equipment monitoring as the project evolves. This adaptive approach ensures continuous protection without the need for constant client intervention to update the security plan.

Our Process

Every construction security engagement begins with a site assessment conducted by our operations team. We walk the property with the project manager or superintendent, identify high-value equipment locations, map access points, evaluate perimeter fencing and lighting, and review the project timeline to anticipate security needs at each phase. Based on this assessment, we develop a security plan that specifies officer schedules, patrol routes, access control protocols, and reporting procedures.

Officers are briefed on the site before their first shift, including introductions to key project personnel and orientation on safety hazards specific to the site. Throughout the project, our management team reviews shift reports, conducts unannounced quality checks, and meets regularly with the client to adjust security operations as the project advances. This proactive management ensures that security keeps pace with construction progress.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can your officers manage gate access for subcontractors? Yes. Our guards verify contractor credentials against an approved access list, log entry and exit times, and ensure that only authorized personnel and vehicles enter the site. We coordinate with the general contractor to keep access lists current.

Do you provide security during active construction hours? We provide security during both active work hours and after-hours periods. Daytime security focuses on access control and material delivery verification, while overnight security focuses on perimeter patrol and equipment protection.

How do you handle security on multi-phase developments? We scale our security program with the project. As new phases open and completed phases close, we adjust officer positioning, patrol routes, and access control points to match the current state of the development.

What reporting do we receive? Clients receive detailed shift reports after every shift, incident reports within one hour of any event, and weekly summaries that identify trends, concerns, and recommendations for improving site security. All reports are available digitally and can be shared with project stakeholders, insurance carriers, and general contractors as needed.

Do your officers enforce OSHA safety requirements? Our officers support site safety compliance by ensuring that unauthorized individuals do not enter hazardous work zones and that visitors are directed to safe areas. While our guards are not OSHA inspectors, they serve as an additional layer of safety awareness on the job site.
