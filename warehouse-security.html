Access Control and Perimeter Security

Controlling access to a warehouse facility is critical for preventing unauthorized entry, tracking personnel movement, and maintaining inventory accountability. U.S.S. Agency officers manage entry gates, verify employee and contractor credentials, log vehicle arrivals and departures, and enforce facility-specific access policies. Our guards monitor loading docks, receiving areas, and employee entrances to ensure that only authorized individuals access restricted zones within the facility.

Perimeter security includes regular patrol of fence lines, exterior walls, and surrounding areas to detect signs of intrusion, tampering, or vulnerability. Our officers identify and report broken fencing, malfunctioning lighting, propped doors, and other conditions that could compromise facility security before they are exploited.

Theft Prevention and Inventory Protection

Internal theft and cargo theft represent significant financial risks for warehouse operators. U.S.S. Agency officers serve as a visible deterrent against employee theft while also monitoring for external threats such as organized cargo theft rings. Our guards conduct vehicle inspections at exit points, monitor high-value storage areas, and verify outbound shipment documentation to prevent unauthorized removal of inventory. These procedures are implemented in coordination with your management team to align with existing loss prevention policies.

Safety Compliance and Emergency Response

Warehouse environments present safety hazards including forklift operations, chemical storage, fire risks, and heavy machinery. U.S.S. Agency officers are trained to identify OSHA-relevant safety violations and report them to facility management. In emergency situations, our guards coordinate evacuation procedures, manage assembly points, and interface with fire departments and emergency medical services to ensure an organized response.

Our Warehouse Security Process

Every warehouse security engagement begins with a comprehensive facility assessment. Our operations team evaluates your property layout, identifies access points and blind spots, reviews existing security systems and procedures, and interviews key personnel to understand operational workflows and known vulnerabilities. This assessment produces a detailed security plan that specifies officer placement, patrol routes, access control protocols, and reporting procedures.

Once the security plan is implemented, U.S.S. Agency provides ongoing oversight through supervisory site visits, officer performance evaluations, and regular client consultations. We review incident data and patrol reports to identify trends and adjust security measures proactively. Clients receive a dedicated account manager who serves as a single point of contact for scheduling, reporting, and service modifications.
