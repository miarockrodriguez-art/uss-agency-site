Protection Without Compromise

The difference between a security company you hire and a security company you trust comes down to one question: who is actually on the post when something happens? U.S.S. Agency was built around the answer. Every officer on our roster is individually vetted, licensed, trained to standard, and accountable to a field lead who knows their name.

We serve businesses, residential communities, executives, events, and sensitive environments. Our clients include corporate headquarters, multi-family apartment complexes, homeowners associations, private schools, hospitals, places of worship, construction sites, retail plazas, and hospitality properties. The U.S.S. Agency Standard

Every officer we deploy is licensed, vetted, and trained to a standard measurably higher than the state minimum. Armed officers hold an active Class G Statewide Firearms License. Unarmed officers hold an active Class D Security Officer License. Field leadership includes certified Law Enforcement Firearms Instructors and personnel with active-duty military experience.

Vetting and licensing

Every officer passes a multi-stage background check, drug screen, and credential verification before placement. Armed officers complete annual firearms requalification. Every license is kept current and on person. If an officer's license lapses, they are off the roster the same day — no exceptions.

Training led by real instructors

Our training is led by certified Law Enforcement Firearms Instructors and seasoned field leadership with active-duty military backgrounds. We reinforce baseline training continually with de-escalation, legal use-of-force training, incident documentation, emergency response protocols, and client-specific site procedures. Most security companies stop at the state minimum. We start there.

Technology you can actually see

Every deployment includes GPS check-in and check-out, digital Daily Activity Reports with photo evidence, real-time incident alerts to the client and account manager, and a client-facing dashboard.

Local field operations

When something happens on your property, the person handling it is local. You don't get routed to a regional call center. You get a field lead who can be on your site in the time it takes to make a coffee.

Licensing and Compliance

U.S.S. Agency operates under Florida Statute Chapter 493, Section 6301 et seq. — the Florida Private Security Services Act — with our Class B Agency License on file with the Florida Department of Agriculture and Consumer Services, Division of Licensing. We carry commercial general liability coverage above the statutory minimum. Certificates of insurance are available to every client on request and are updated annually. Every officer carries their individual Class D and Class G license on person while on duty, verifiable through FDACS at any time.

What qualifications do your armed officers have?

+

Every armed officer deployed by U.S.S. Agency holds an active Florida Class G Statewide Firearms License, issued by the Florida Department of Agriculture and Consumer Services after completion of a state-approved 28-hour firearms training program and live-fire qualification. Before they are scheduled for post, each officer also passes FDLE-level fingerprinting, a multi-state criminal background check, drug screening, and an internal interview. Many of our armed officers bring additional credentials — active or retired law enforcement, military police, or federal protective service experience. U.S.S. Agency maintains ongoing training in use-of-force decision-making, de-escalation, emergency medical response, crowd control, and annual firearms re-qualification. License numbers, training records, and insurance certificates are available on request for any corporate or government client who requires vendor compliance documentation. Agency License: B2800082 (Florida).

How are security service rates determined?

+

U.S.S. Agency security rates are quoted per assignment rather than a flat list price, because the real cost depends on variables specific to each site. The main rate drivers are: armed versus unarmed officer classification, number of hours and officers required per shift, whether coverage is a one-time event or an ongoing contract, total weekly hours (which often qualify for volume discounts), patrol vehicle or GPS tour requirements, on-site supervisor or dispatch needs, location and travel time, overnight or holiday premium hours, and any specialty credentials required such as off-duty law enforcement or executive-protection-trained personnel. Event security and short-notice deployments typically carry a minimum-hour commitment. Every quote is transparent, itemized, and delivered in writing. Call 321-304-2430 or request a free consultation for a written rate specific to your Florida property, event, or corporate site.

What are plainclothes security guards and when are they needed?

+

Plainclothes security guards are licensed officers who provide protection in everyday civilian attire instead of a visible uniform, allowing them to blend into a location without drawing attention. U.S.S. Agency deploys plainclothes officers for executive protection assignments, corporate and family office security, retail loss prevention and organized-theft surveillance, high-net-worth residential coverage, sensitive workplace investigations, hotel and resort VIP details, and discreet event security where a uniformed presence would disrupt the atmosphere or tip off a threat. Plainclothes officers still carry full legal authority — armed when required under Florida Class G license — and coordinate with on-site management, local law enforcement, and uniformed perimeter officers when combined coverage is needed. This option is especially common for tech companies, financial firms, VIP clients, and clients managing internal investigations. Available statewide throughout Florida.

Why hire off-duty law enforcement?

+

Hiring off-duty law enforcement through U.S.S. Agency gives clients a level of authority and credibility that standard armed security cannot match. Off-duty officers are actively sworn peace officers with arrest authority, current agency training, and direct radio coordination with local police when needed. They are especially valuable for high-profile corporate events, political fundraisers and campaign appearances, crowd-control scenarios, VIP travel, entertainment industry shoots, civil-dispute standbys, and any situation where the visible presence of law enforcement immediately de-escalates risk. U.S.S. Agency coordinates off-duty assignments through each officer's employing agency to ensure full compliance with their department's moonlighting policy, and we carry general liability coverage on every engagement. Clients receive officer selection based on assignment fit — tactical, protective, or uniformed presence — throughout Orlando, Miami, Tampa, Jacksonville, and statewide Florida.
