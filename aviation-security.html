Security for the Aviation Industry

Aviation facilities require security programs that meet the stringent standards of the FAA, TSA, and airport authorities while addressing threats unique to airfield operations. U.S.S. Agency provides armed and unarmed security officers for airports, fixed-base operators, private hangars, cargo facilities, and aviation maintenance operations throughout Florida. Our officers are trained in aviation-specific security protocols, airfield safety procedures, and the regulatory compliance requirements that govern access to aviation facilities.

As a licensed security agency in Florida, U.S.S. Agency serves general aviation airports, regional airfields, cargo terminals, and private aviation facilities with security programs designed to protect aircraft, passengers, employees, and infrastructure. Our guards understand the critical importance of perimeter security, access control, and threat identification in aviation environments where the consequences of a security breach can be catastrophic.

Airfield Access Control and Perimeter Security

Preventing unauthorized access to airside areas is the primary objective of aviation security. U.S.S. Agency officers manage gates, checkpoints, and access points that separate public areas from restricted airfield operations. Our guards verify credentials, monitor badge access systems, escort visitors and contractors in secured areas, and conduct perimeter patrols to detect breaches in fencing or unauthorized entry points. For general aviation airports without TSA-level screening, our security presence fills a critical gap in access control.

FBO and Private Aviation Security

Fixed-base operators and private aviation terminals serve high-net-worth individuals, corporate flight departments, and charter operations that require discreet, professional security services. U.S.S. Agency officers provide lobby security, ramp access control, aircraft monitoring, and VIP arrival and departure coordination at FBO facilities. Our guards understand the customer service expectations of private aviation while maintaining the security awareness necessary to protect aircraft, passengers, and sensitive cargo.

Cargo and Hangar Facility Protection

Aviation cargo facilities and maintenance hangars contain high-value aircraft, parts, and shipments that require protection from theft and tampering. U.S.S. Agency officers patrol hangar areas, monitor cargo handling operations, verify personnel credentials, and secure facilities during off-hours. Our guards also support compliance with known shipper programs and cargo security requirements established by federal aviation authorities.
