High-Risk Employee Termination Security

Terminating an employee who has displayed threatening behavior, made direct or veiled threats, or has a documented history of hostility creates a situation where the safety of HR personnel, managers, and coworkers is at risk. U.S.S. Agency provides on-site armed security presence during high-risk terminations, ensuring that the process is conducted safely, professionally, and with full control of the environment. Our armed security guards in Orlando are trained specifically for these high-tension scenarios and understand how to project authority while maintaining a calm, controlled atmosphere.

Our officers arrive before the termination meeting, conduct a security assessment of the space, position themselves strategically, and remain on site through the entire process including the employee's departure from the premises. We coordinate with HR, legal counsel, and management to establish clear protocols for the meeting, including escort procedures, property retrieval, access revocation, and post-termination monitoring of the building perimeter. This level of preparation protects your people and demonstrates your organization's commitment to employee safety.

Hostile Workplace Intervention

When workplace conflict escalates beyond the capacity of management to control, professional security intervention becomes necessary to protect employees and restore order. U.S.S. Agency provides armed and unarmed officers who respond to hostile workplace situations with the training and composure required to de-escalate confrontations, separate hostile parties, and establish a safe environment for all employees. Our officers work alongside management to assess the situation, implement immediate safety measures, and develop a plan for ongoing security coverage if the threat persists.

Hostile workplace situations can arise from interpersonal conflicts, disciplinary actions, layoffs, organizational changes, or external threats such as domestic violence that follows an employee into the workplace. U.S.S. Agency officers are trained to recognize the warning signs of escalating violence and to intervene at the earliest possible stage. As a licensed security agency in Florida, we bring the legal knowledge, tactical training, and professional judgment that these sensitive situations demand.

Threat Assessment and De-Escalation

Preventing workplace violence begins with identifying threats before they materialize into action. U.S.S. Agency provides threat assessment services that evaluate the credibility and severity of threats made by current employees, former employees, customers, vendors, or other individuals connected to your organization. Our assessment process examines the individual's statements, behavior patterns, access to weapons, history of violence, and the specific circumstances that triggered the threatening behavior.

Our officers are trained in verbal de-escalation techniques that reduce tension, redirect aggressive behavior, and create space for resolution without physical confrontation. De-escalation is not a passive approach. It is a deliberate, trained response that requires officers who can read behavioral cues, communicate effectively under pressure, and maintain control of a situation through professional presence and calm authority. U.S.S. Agency armed guard services include officers who bring this critical capability to every assignment.

Post-Termination Monitoring

The risk of workplace violence does not end when the terminated employee leaves the building. In many cases, the greatest risk occurs in the days and weeks following a high-risk termination when the individual has had time to process the situation and potentially plan a retaliatory action. U.S.S. Agency provides post-termination security coverage that includes increased security presence at the workplace, monitoring of building access points, parking lot patrols, and coordination with law enforcement if the threat level warrants it.

We work with your management team to establish a monitoring timeline based on the threat assessment and adjust coverage as the situation evolves. This ongoing protection gives your employees the confidence to return to work without fear and demonstrates your organization's commitment to maintaining a safe workplace.

Corporate Violence Prevention Programs

Reactive security is important, but proactive violence prevention programs are what truly protect organizations from workplace violence incidents. U.S.S. Agency works with corporate clients to develop comprehensive workplace violence prevention programs that include threat reporting procedures, incident response protocols, employee awareness training, and security infrastructure assessments. These programs establish a framework that enables your organization to identify, report, and respond to potential threats before they escalate into violent incidents.
