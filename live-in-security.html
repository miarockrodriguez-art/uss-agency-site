Continuous On-Premises Protection

Live-in security represents the highest level of residential protection available. Unlike shift-based security where officers rotate on and off the property, live-in security officers reside at the client's estate or property, providing an uninterrupted security presence that eliminates coverage gaps entirely. U.S.S. Agency provides live-in armed and unarmed security professionals for private estates, luxury residences, and high-net-worth individuals throughout Florida.

As a licensed security agency in Florida, U.S.S. Agency selects live-in security officers through an elevated vetting process that goes beyond standard background checks. Live-in officers must demonstrate exceptional judgment, discretion, professionalism, and interpersonal skills because they operate within the client's personal living environment. Every officer we assign to a live-in post is carefully matched to the client's specific requirements, household dynamics, and security needs.

Estate Security and Property Management Support

Large estates with multiple buildings, extensive grounds, and numerous access points require security officers who know every inch of the property. Live-in security officers from U.S.S. Agency develop intimate knowledge of the estate's layout, routines, vulnerabilities, and personnel. This familiarity allows them to detect anomalies that a rotating shift officer might miss, whether it is an unfamiliar vehicle on a service road, a gate left unsecured, or a change in a neighbor's behavior pattern.

Beyond traditional security duties, our live-in officers often support property management functions such as accepting deliveries, coordinating service provider access, monitoring alarm systems, and managing gate operations. This dual-role capability makes live-in security an efficient solution for clients who want comprehensive property oversight from a trusted professional.

Personal and Family Protection

For high-net-worth individuals and families, live-in security provides a level of personal protection that extends beyond the property perimeter. Our officers are trained to manage daily security logistics including vehicle inspections, route planning, visitor screening, and emergency evacuation procedures for all family members. The constant presence of a trained security professional provides peace of mind that cannot be replicated by cameras, alarms, or part-time security guards.

Discretion and Confidentiality

Live-in security officers operate within the most private spaces of a client's life. U.S.S. Agency enforces strict confidentiality agreements with all live-in personnel and maintains rigorous standards for discretion, professional boundaries, and client privacy. Our officers understand that their role is to protect without intruding, maintaining a professional presence that integrates into the household without disrupting the client's lifestyle or family dynamic.

Our Live-In Security Process

Every live-in security engagement begins with a confidential consultation where we assess the client's security needs, property characteristics, family dynamics, lifestyle patterns, and specific concerns. This assessment informs the development of a comprehensive residential security plan that covers daily operations, emergency procedures, visitor management protocols, and communication standards.

We then select officer candidates whose experience, personality, and capabilities match the assignment profile. Clients have the opportunity to interview and approve their live-in officer before the assignment begins. Once the officer is placed, an integration period allows the officer to learn the property layout, establish routines, meet household staff and family members, and calibrate security operations to the client's expectations. Ongoing oversight from our management team ensures that service quality remains consistent throughout the engagement.

Frequently Asked Questions

What accommodations are required for a live-in security officer? Live-in officers require private sleeping quarters on or adjacent to the property, such as a guest house, guest suite, or dedicated room with bathroom access. The specific accommodation requirements are discussed during the initial consultation and tailored to each property's available facilities.

How are shift schedules managed for live-in officers? Live-in officers follow structured on-duty and off-duty schedules to ensure alertness and sustained performance. For properties requiring true 24/7 active coverage, U.S.S. Agency assigns rotating officers so that active security operations never rely on a single fatigued individual. The scheduling model is determined during planning based on the client's coverage requirements.

Can live-in officers travel with the family? Yes. Many live-in security assignments include travel protection as part of the scope of service. Officers accompany clients on domestic and international travel, providing continuous protection that extends beyond the property boundaries. Travel security logistics including advance planning and coordination are managed by U.S.S. Agency.

How do you ensure the officer is the right fit for my household? U.S.S. Agency presents pre-screened officer candidates for client review and approval before any placement. We consider personality compatibility, communication style, relevant experience, and cultural alignment alongside tactical qualifications. If the initial placement does not meet expectations, we arrange a replacement at no additional cost.

What is the minimum engagement period for live-in security? Engagement periods vary based on client needs. U.S.S. Agency accommodates both short-term assignments, such as protecting a property during an extended absence, and long-term placements that continue indefinitely. Minimum terms and pricing are discussed during the initial consultation.
