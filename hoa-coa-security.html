Security Built for Community Associations

Homeowner associations and condominium owner associations carry a legal and fiduciary responsibility to protect the safety and property values of their communities. U.S.S. Agency partners with HOA and COA boards throughout Orlando and Central Florida to deliver security programs that fulfill that responsibility with professionalism, consistency, and full compliance with Florida statutes. As a licensed security agency in Florida, we understand the regulatory environment that governs community associations and tailor our services accordingly.

Our security officers serve as an extension of your board's commitment to community safety. Whether your association requires gate attendants, roving patrols, or armed security guards for higher-risk communities, U.S.S. Agency provides trained personnel who represent your community with the professionalism residents expect.

Gatehouse Staffing and Access Management

For gated communities, the gatehouse is the first line of defense. U.S.S. Agency provides trained gate attendants who verify visitor credentials, manage resident access lists, log contractor and delivery entries, and enforce community-specific access policies. Our officers maintain detailed records that provide boards with full visibility into who enters and exits the community at all times. We coordinate directly with property management companies to ensure access procedures align with community rules and are consistently enforced across every shift.

Beyond standard gatehouse operations, our officers are trained to identify suspicious vehicles, handle tailgating situations at vehicle gates, and manage emergency access for first responders. This level of attention to access control reduces unauthorized entries, deters criminal activity, and gives residents confidence in their community's security posture.

Community Patrol and Rule Enforcement

HOA and COA communities include shared amenities, common areas, and public spaces that require regular monitoring. U.S.S. Agency officers conduct vehicle and foot patrols throughout the community on scheduled and randomized routes designed to maximize coverage and visibility. Patrol activities include monitoring pools and clubhouses, checking parking areas for unauthorized vehicles, identifying maintenance hazards, and documenting violations of community rules for board review.

Our officers understand the difference between law enforcement and community security. They are trained to enforce community guidelines through professional communication and documentation rather than confrontation. When situations escalate, our guards follow established protocols that prioritize de-escalation while protecting the safety of all residents.

Board Reporting and Transparency

Community association boards need detailed, accurate reporting to make informed decisions about security operations. U.S.S. Agency provides comprehensive shift reports, incident documentation, and activity summaries that give board members and property managers complete transparency into security operations. Our reporting includes visitor logs, patrol records, incident narratives, and trend analysis that helps boards identify patterns and allocate security resources effectively.
