State-Certified Armed Security Officers

U.S.S. Agency provides highly trained armed security guards who carry the authority, discipline, and tactical readiness required for the most demanding security assignments. Every armed officer on our roster holds a valid Florida Class G Statewide Firearms License, issued by the Florida Department of Agriculture and Consumer Services. This means each guard has completed rigorous firearms training, passed state-mandated background checks, and demonstrated proficiency in the safe handling and use of firearms while on duty.

Our armed security guards are not simply personnel with a firearm. They are professionals who understand threat assessment, situational awareness, de-escalation techniques, and the legal framework governing the use of force in the State of Florida. As a licensed security agency in Florida, U.S.S. Agency maintains strict internal standards that exceed state minimums, ensuring our clients receive armed guard services that reflect the highest level of professionalism in the industry.

Our Training and Qualification Standards

The Florida Class G license represents the minimum legal threshold for armed security work. U.S.S. Agency treats it as a starting point, not a standard. Our armed officers complete additional internal training that covers advanced marksmanship, low-light shooting scenarios, weapon retention techniques, tactical communication, and force decision-making under stress. We conduct regular firearms qualification shoots and performance evaluations to ensure that every armed officer on our roster maintains the skill level our clients depend on.

Beyond firearms proficiency, our armed guards are trained in first aid, CPR, AED operation, and emergency response coordination. In a critical incident, the ability to render aid, direct evacuations, and interface with arriving first responders can be as important as the ability to engage a threat. U.S.S. Agency armed officers are prepared for the full spectrum of emergency scenarios, not just the ones that require a firearm.

Our Process

Engaging U.S.S. Agency for armed security begins with a comprehensive threat assessment of your property or operation. Our management team evaluates the physical environment, identifies potential threat vectors, reviews incident history, and assesses the level of armed response capability required. Based on this assessment, we develop a security plan that specifies the number of armed officers, their positioning, shift schedules, rules of engagement, and coordination protocols with law enforcement.

Officers assigned to your account are selected based on their experience, tactical capability, and suitability for your specific environment. A financial institution requires a different officer profile than a construction site or a private estate. We match the right personnel to the right assignment, brief them thoroughly on the site and its protocols, and deploy with full operational readiness. Our management team maintains ongoing oversight through shift report reviews, site inspections, and regular client communication.

Frequently Asked Questions

What firearms do your armed officers carry? Our armed officers carry duty-grade handguns that are inspected and approved by our agency. All firearms comply with Florida Department of Agriculture and Consumer Services regulations for licensed security officers. Specific firearm selections are based on the assignment requirements and officer qualification.

What insurance coverage do your armed guards carry? U.S.S. Agency maintains comprehensive general liability insurance and professional liability coverage that extends to all armed security operations. Our insurance program is designed to protect both our clients and our officers in the event of an incident.

Can armed guards be deployed on short notice? Yes. We maintain a roster of qualified armed officers who can be deployed within 24 to 48 hours for urgent assignments. For planned engagements, we recommend a lead time of 72 hours to allow for site orientation and officer briefing.

How do your armed officers handle use-of-force situations? Our officers follow a strict use-of-force continuum that prioritizes de-escalation, verbal commands, and non-physical resolution whenever possible. Firearms are a last resort deployed only when there is an imminent threat of death or serious bodily harm. Every use-of-force incident is documented, reported, and reviewed by management.
