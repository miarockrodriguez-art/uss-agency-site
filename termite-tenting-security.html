Protecting Vacant Properties During Fumigation

When a property undergoes termite tenting and fumigation, it must be completely vacated for two to three days while toxic gases treat the structure. During this period, the property is unoccupied, doors may be unsealed for ventilation, and personal belongings, appliances, and valuables remain inside without the protection of alarm systems or residents. This creates a window of vulnerability that criminals exploit for burglary, theft, and vandalism. U.S.S. Agency provides dedicated security guards for properties undergoing termite tenting throughout Orlando and Central Florida.

As a licensed security agency in Florida, U.S.S. Agency understands the fumigation process and the specific security risks it creates. Our officers are trained to maintain safe distances from tented structures while providing continuous surveillance and access control throughout the entire tenting period. We coordinate with pest control companies to ensure our security operations do not interfere with the fumigation process.

Continuous Surveillance Throughout the Tenting Process

Termite tenting typically requires 48 to 72 hours of treatment time during which the property must remain secured. U.S.S. Agency officers maintain a continuous security presence at the property from the moment tenting begins until the pest control company clears the structure and the homeowner returns. Our guards monitor the property perimeter, watch for unauthorized individuals attempting to enter the structure, and document all activity around the property throughout the tenting period.

Criminals specifically target tented properties because the tenting itself signals that the home is vacant and that alarm systems have been deactivated. Our visible security presence eliminates this opportunity by demonstrating that the property is actively monitored despite being unoccupied.

Liability Protection for Pest Control Companies

Pest control companies that perform fumigation services face significant liability exposure if unauthorized individuals enter a tented structure and are exposed to lethal fumigation gases. U.S.S. Agency security officers protect pest control companies from this liability by preventing unauthorized entry during the active tenting period. Our presence and documentation provide evidence that reasonable security measures were in place throughout the fumigation process, protecting both the pest control company and the property owner from potential claims.
