Protecting Vacant Properties During Fumigation

When a property undergoes termite tenting and fumigation, it must be completely vacated for two to three days while toxic gases treat the structure. During this period, the property is unoccupied, doors may be unsealed for ventilation, and personal belongings, appliances, and valuables remain inside without the protection of alarm systems or residents. This creates a window of vulnerability that criminals exploit for burglary, theft, and vandalism. U.S.S. Agency provides dedicated security guards for properties undergoing termite tenting throughout Orlando and Central Florida.

As a licensed security agency in Florida, U.S.S. Agency understands the fumigation process and the specific security risks it creates. Our officers are trained to maintain safe distances from tented structures while providing continuous surveillance and access control throughout the entire tenting period. We coordinate with pest control companies to ensure our security operations do not interfere with the fumigation process.

Continuous Surveillance Throughout the Tenting Process

Termite tenting typically requires 48 to 72 hours of treatment time during which the property must remain secured. U.S.S. Agency officers maintain a continuous security presence at the property from the moment tenting begins until the pest control company clears the structure and the homeowner returns. Our guards monitor the property perimeter, watch for unauthorized individuals attempting to enter the structure, and document all activity around the property throughout the tenting period.

Criminals specifically target tented properties because the tenting itself signals that the home is vacant and that alarm systems have been deactivated. Our visible security presence eliminates this opportunity by demonstrating that the property is actively monitored despite being unoccupied.

Liability Protection for Pest Control Companies

Pest control companies that perform fumigation services face significant liability exposure if unauthorized individuals enter a tented structure and are exposed to lethal fumigation gases. U.S.S. Agency security officers protect pest control companies from this liability by preventing unauthorized entry during the active tenting period. Our presence and documentation provide evidence that reasonable security measures were in place throughout the fumigation process, protecting both the pest control company and the property owner from potential claims.

Our Tenting Security Process

When a property owner or pest control company engages U.S.S. Agency for termite tenting security, our process begins with a pre-tenting site visit. We assess the property layout, identify all potential access points, evaluate lighting and visibility conditions, and coordinate with the pest control company to understand their specific tenting timeline and procedures. This information is used to develop a security plan that specifies officer positioning, patrol routes around the perimeter, and communication protocols with the pest control team.

On the day tenting begins, our officer arrives before the tent is fully sealed and remains on-site continuously until the property is cleared for reoccupation. Throughout the tenting period, the officer maintains a detailed log of all activity around the property, including pedestrian traffic, vehicle activity, weather conditions, and any approaches to the structure. At the conclusion of the tenting period, we provide the property owner with a comprehensive security report covering the entire duration of the fumigation.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does termite tenting security typically last? Most residential fumigation operations require 48 to 72 hours of continuous security coverage. Larger commercial properties may require longer coverage periods. U.S.S. Agency provides uninterrupted security for the entire duration of the tenting process, regardless of length.

Do your officers stay on the property overnight? Yes. Our officers provide 24/7 coverage throughout the tenting period, including overnight hours when the risk of unauthorized entry is highest. Officers work in shifts to maintain alertness and effectiveness around the clock.

Can you coordinate directly with my pest control company? Yes. U.S.S. Agency regularly coordinates with pest control companies to align security coverage with the fumigation schedule. We work with the pest control team to understand tent sealing procedures, ventilation timelines, and clearance protocols so our security operations support rather than interfere with the fumigation process.

Is tenting security required by law? While not mandated by law in most jurisdictions, termite tenting security is strongly recommended by pest control industry associations and insurance carriers. The liability exposure created by an unguarded tented property, particularly the risk of fatal exposure to fumigation gases, makes professional security a prudent investment.

How far in advance should I schedule tenting security? We recommend scheduling security as soon as your fumigation date is confirmed. Most clients book one to two weeks in advance. U.S.S. Agency can accommodate shorter notice periods when officer availability permits.
