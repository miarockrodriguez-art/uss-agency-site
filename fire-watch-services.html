Code-Compliant Fire Watch Coverage

When a building's fire suppression or detection system is impaired, out of service, or not yet installed, fire codes require a trained fire watch officer to be on site continuously until the system is restored. U.S.S. Agency provides certified fire watch guards for buildings, construction sites, and facilities throughout Orlando and Central Florida. Our fire watch officers are trained in fire hazard identification, patrol documentation, and emergency notification procedures that satisfy fire marshal requirements and building code compliance.

As a licensed security agency in Florida, U.S.S. Agency deploys fire watch officers on short notice to help property owners, construction managers, and facility operators avoid code violations, fines, and the catastrophic risk of an unmonitored fire event. Whether your sprinkler system is down for maintenance, your fire alarm is being repaired, or your construction project has not yet received fire system approval, our fire watch services keep you compliant and protected.

Construction Site Fire Watch

Construction sites frequently require fire watch services during hot work operations such as welding, cutting, brazing, and soldering. OSHA and NFPA standards mandate that a fire watch be maintained during and after hot work to monitor for smoldering materials and prevent fires from igniting in construction debris, insulation, or adjacent combustible materials. U.S.S. Agency fire watch officers are trained in hot work fire watch procedures and maintain vigilant observation of the work area for the required duration after hot work operations conclude.

New construction projects also require fire watch when buildings are occupied before fire protection systems are fully installed and inspected. Our officers provide the continuous monitoring that allows construction schedules to proceed while maintaining compliance with fire codes and occupancy requirements.

Building System Impairment Fire Watch

When a building's sprinkler system, fire alarm system, or standpipe is taken out of service for repair, testing, or maintenance, the fire code requires fire watch coverage until the system is restored. U.S.S. Agency provides fire watch officers who patrol the affected areas on documented schedules, monitor for signs of fire or smoke, and maintain direct communication with the fire department in the event of an emergency. Our detailed patrol logs and documentation satisfy fire marshal inspection requirements and provide property owners with proof of compliance.

Emergency and Same-Day Deployment

Fire watch needs often arise without warning when a system fails, a pipe breaks, or a fire marshal issues a compliance order. U.S.S. Agency maintains a roster of certified fire watch officers available for same-day and emergency deployment throughout Central Florida. We understand that every hour without fire watch coverage represents both a compliance violation and a safety risk, and we staff accordingly to respond to urgent requests.
